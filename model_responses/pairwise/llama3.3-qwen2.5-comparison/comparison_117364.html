The formation of multi-longitudinal swirls facilitates the mixing of hot and
cold fluids within the tube, thereby thinning the temperature boundary layer
adjacent to the wall and promoting a more uniform temperature distribution. This
enhanced mixing improves convective heat transfer between the tube wall and the
fluid, allowing heat to be more rapidly transferred from the wall zone to the
core-flow zone. Comparative analysis of the N-SS and W-SS bionic rib-enhanced
tubes reveals that the vortex core of the longitudinal swirls generated by the
W-SS bionic rib is positioned farther from the wall. This configuration disrupts
the velocity profile in the core-flow zone, leading to more intense mixing and a
more uniform temperature distribution within the W-SS enhanced tube.
Consequently, the multi-longitudinal swirls induced by the bionic ribs enhance
the mixing of the cold fluid in the core-flow zone with the hot fluid in the
boundary-flow zone, reducing the temperature gradient and thereby enhancing the
overall heat transfer capability of the fluid.
