I think there's room for improvement.

While it's great that you're enthusiastic and willing to take a chance, the tone
of this email comes across as a bit too casual and uncertain. Here's why:

  1. "I don't know if you're hiring" - This phrase can make it seem like you haven't done your research or taken the time to understand the company's current needs.
  2. "Wasn't sure if you're the correct person" - This suggests that you haven't
    taken the time to find the right contact or do your due diligence on the
    company.
  3. "Facebooker" - This is not a word, and it's likely you meant to say
    "Facebook" or "I saw your company on Facebook."
  4. "Figured what the heck" - This phrase is too casual and informal for a job
    application email. It can come across as unprofessional and lacking in
    seriousness.

Here's a suggested rewrite:

"Dear [Hiring Manager's Name],

I came across [Company Name] on Facebook and was impressed by the work you do.
I'm excited to learn more about potential opportunities at your company.
Although I couldn't find any current job postings, I wanted to reach out and
express my interest in exploring possible roles that may be available.

I've attached my resume for your review. If you're not the right person to
contact, please let me know who I should reach out to. I'm looking forward to
the opportunity to discuss my qualifications further.

Best regards,
[Your Name]"

This rewritten email:

  • Shows that you've done some research on the company
  • Expresses your interest in the company and potential opportunities
  • Is more formal and professional in tone
  • Includes a clear call to action (attaching your resume and requesting a response)
