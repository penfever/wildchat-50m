Amazon in 1995:
In July 1995, Amazon launched as an online bookstore with a simple website. The
site was created by Jeff Bezos, and it was initially called Cadabra, Inc. (later
changed to Amazon.com, Inc.). The website had a basic design, with a focus on
functionality rather than aesthetics. Some notable features of Amazon's 1995
website include:

  • A plain, text-based interface with a blue background
  • A simple search bar and basic navigation
  • A limited selection of books (around 90,000 titles)
  • No images or graphics, except for a small Amazon logo
  • A "1-Click Ordering" system, which allowed customers to store their shipping and payment information for future purchases

Amazon in 1997:
By 1997, Amazon had expanded its product offerings beyond books to include CDs,
videos, and software. The website underwent significant changes to accommodate
the growing product catalog and improving technology. Some notable features of
Amazon's 1997 website include:

  • A more modern design with a white background and a larger, more prominent logo
  • Improved navigation, including a category-based menu and a "Browse" section
  • The introduction of images and graphics, such as product thumbnails and icons
  • A "Recommendations" feature, which suggested products based on customers' browsing and purchasing history
  • An expanded product selection, with over 1 million items in stock
  • The launch of Amazon's associates program, which allowed website owners to earn commissions by promoting Amazon products

Key differences:
Comparing the two websites, we can see significant improvements in design,
functionality, and features between 1995 and 1997. Some key differences include:

  • Design: The 1997 website has a more modern and visually appealing design, with a cleaner layout and more graphics.
  • Navigation: The 1997 website has improved navigation, making it easier for customers to find what they're looking for.
  • Product selection: The 1997 website offers a much broader selection of products, with over 1 million items in stock.
  • Personalization: The 1997 website introduces personalized recommendations, which would become a hallmark of Amazon's customer experience.
  • Partnerships: The 1997 website launches the associates program, which would help Amazon expand its reach and build a network of partners.

Overall, the evolution of Amazon's website between 1995 and 1997 reflects the
company's rapid growth, improving technology, and increasing focus on customer
