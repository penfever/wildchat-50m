When a satellite in a circular orbit around the Earth experiences a decrease in its speed, several changes occur due to the principles of orbital mechanics. Let's analyze this step-by-step:

1. Orbital Mechanics Basics:

  • Kepler's Laws: These laws describe the motion of planets and satellites. For a circular orbit, the satellite's speed ( v ) is given by: [ v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}} ] where ( G ) is the gravitational constant, ( M ) is the mass of the Earth, and ( r ) is the orbital radius.

  • Centripetal Force: The centripetal force required to keep the satellite in orbit is provided by the gravitational force: [ \frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{GMm}{r^2} ] Simplifying, we get: [ v^2 = \frac{GM}{r} ]

2. Effect of Decreased Speed:

  • If the speed ( v ) decreases, the right-hand side of the equation ( v^2 = \frac{GM}{r} ) must also decrease to maintain equilibrium. This implies that the orbital radius ( r ) must increase to balance the equation.

3. Energy Considerations:

  • The total mechanical energy ( E ) of the satellite in a circular orbit is the sum of its kinetic energy ( K ) and potential energy ( U ): [ E = K + U = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 - \frac{GMm}{r} ]
  • For a circular orbit, the total energy is: [ E = -\frac{GMm}{2r} ]
  • If the speed decreases, the kinetic energy ( K ) decreases. To conserve energy, the potential energy ( U ) must also change. Since the potential energy is negative and becomes less negative (i.e., more positive), the satellite moves to a higher orbit where the potential energy is less negative.

4. Orbital Radius Increase:

  • As the satellite slows down, it no longer has enough kinetic energy to maintain its original circular orbit. It starts to move into an elliptical orbit with a higher apogee (farthest point from the Earth). Over time, if the speed remains lower, the satellite will settle into a new circular orbit at a higher altitude.

5. Period of Revolution:

  • The period ( T ) of a satellite in a circular orbit is given by Kepler's third law: [ T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{r^3}{GM}} ]
  • As the orbital radius ( r ) increases, the period ( T ) also increases because the satellite has to travel a longer distance in its orbit.

Conclusion:

  • Orbital Radius: The orbital radius of the satellite will increase.
  • Period of Revolution: The period of revolution of the satellite will increase.

These changes are consistent with the principles of conservation of energy and the relationship between orbital speed, radius, and period as described by Kepler's laws and Newtonian mechanics.
